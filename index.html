Algorithms

Scheduling Algorithms

Compare and contrast five fundamental CPU scheduling strategies.

01

FCFS

First Come First Serve

Processes execute in order of arrival. Simple and fair, but may cause the convoy effect where short processes wait behind long ones.

Non-Preemptive FIFO Queue
02

SJF

Shortest Job First

Selects the process with the shortest burst time. Optimal for minimizing average waiting time but requires knowing burst times in advance.

Non-Preemptive Optimal WT
03

SRTF

Shortest Remaining Time First

Preemptive SJF — a new arrival with shorter remaining time can interrupt the running process. Gives lowest average waiting time.

Preemptive Context Switches
04

Round Robin

Time-Quantum Based

Each process gets a fixed time quantum. If not finished, it goes to the back of the queue. Fair and ideal for time-sharing systems.

Preemptive Fair Sharing
05

Priority

Priority-Based Scheduling

Processes with higher priority (lower number) execute first. Can cause starvation of low-priority processes without aging.

Non-Preemptive Starvation Risk

How It Works

Get started in 4 simple steps.

1

Add Processes

Enter arrival time, burst time, and priority for each process. Click "Add Process" to queue them up. Pre-loaded samples included!

2

Choose Algorithm

Select from FCFS, SJF, SRTF, Round Robin, or Priority scheduling. Adjust quantum for Round Robin.

3

Hit Start

Watch the simulation unfold with animated state transitions, a live transition log, and a Gantt chart building in real-time.

4

Analyze Results

Review average waiting time, turnaround time, throughput, and per-process metrics in the results panel.
